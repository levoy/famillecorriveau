  4. Forget known as Latour and Laforge

    5. 2-9807462-5-8, Forget dit Latour, Serge Corriveau, 05/05/2005, $75.00

    I have also completed a search and recorded weddings of other families to whom I am closely related.

    As such, I have recorded weddings, births and deaths of the Forget/Latour and Laforge families to which I am related on my mother's side who was born to Octavien Forget and Marie Louise Chartier from Sainte Elisabeth, Joliette County, Province of Quebec.

    She is a descendant of Pierre Latour known as Laforge who wedded Etiennette Banliac on December 1st, 1705 at Ile du Pas. She was the daugther of Francois Banliac known as Lamontagne and Marie-Angélique Pelletier. I have recorded more than 6,750 descendants within 935 families for this ancestor.

    This book can be consulted at the Quebec's Great Library in Montreal as well as the Quebec and Canadian national archives.
    This book can also be purchased at a cost of $75 plus postage handling with the author.


  5. Jean Berloin known as Nantel

    6. 978-2-9811014-0-2, Jean Berloin dit Nantel, Serge Corriveau, 06/10/2004, $75.00

    This man was originally from Saint-Maxient, Poitier, France. He married Marguerite Forget on November 7, 1694 at Saint-Francois de Sales, Ile Jésus. They had five children. He married his second wife, Marguerite Belair Delpeche, September 14, 1705 at Ile Jesus and had six children. He died November 14, 1740.

    I have recorded 3,200 descendants for this ancestor including my wife.

    This book can be consulted at the Quebec's Great Library in Montreal as well as the Quebec and Canadian national archives.
    This book can also be purchased at a cost of $75 plus postage handling with the author.


  6. Senécal, Adrien and Jean.

    7. 978-2-9807462-9-1, Les Senécal, Serge Corriveau, 27/11/2008, $75.00

    The book on the descendants of Senecals of America was published at the beginning of December 2008.
    Three Senecals came from France to populate New France. First, Louise arrives from Rouen, Normandie, she belongs to the Guilbault descendants of America. Then comes Adrien in 1669, he is from Bénouville, near Rouen, Normandie. Lastly, Jean arrives from Saint-Martin de Paluel, not far from Dieppe, Normandie, approximately in 1670. I assume the three are related as they all came from the same region in France.

    This book can be consulted at the Quebec and Canadian national archives.
    This book can also be purchased at a cost of $75 plus postage handling with the author.
